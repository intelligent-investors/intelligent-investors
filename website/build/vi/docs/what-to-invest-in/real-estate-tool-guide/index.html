Real Estate Calculator Guide

Buying a house is one of the most significant financial decisions you will make. Understanding how much you will need to pay your bank each month when borrowing money for a home is crucial for effective financial planning. This guide will walk you through the steps to calculate your monthly mortgage payment, ensuring you are well-prepared for homeownership.

Input​

  • Loan Amount: The total amount you are borrowing from the bank.
  • Interest Rate (%): The annual interest rate charged by the bank.
  • Loan Term (in months): The length of time over which the loan will be repaid.

Output​

  • Total Monthly Payment: The amount you will pay each month, including both principal and interest.

Monthly Payment Calculation​

Monthly Principal Payment​

The monthly principal payment is calculated by dividing the total loan amount by the loan term (the number of months over which the loan is repaid).

Principal Payment=Loan AmountLoan Term\text{Principal Payment} = \frac{\text{Loan Amount}}{\text{Loan Term}}Principal Payment=Loan TermLoan Amount​

The interest payment is determined using the remaining loan balance and applying the annual interest rate, then dividing by 12 to find the monthly interest amount:

Interest Payment=(Remaining Balance×Annual Interest Rate12)\text{Interest Payment} = \left(\frac{\text{Remaining Balance} \times \text{Annual Interest Rate}}{12}\right)Interest Payment=(12Remaining Balance×Annual Interest Rate​)

Total Monthly Payment​

The total monthly payment is the sum of the principal and interest payments:

Total Monthly Payment=Principal Payment+Interest Payment\text{Total Monthly Payment} = \text{Principal Payment} + \text{Interest Payment}Total Monthly Payment=Principal Payment+Interest Payment

Example​

Suppose you take a loan for 100,000,000 USD\text{100,000,000 USD}100,000,000 USD with an interest rate of 8%8\%8% per annum and a loan term of 3 years\text{3 years}3 years (36 months\text{36 months}36 months).

Step 1: Calculate Monthly Principal Payment​

Using the formula for the Monthly Principal Payment:

Principal Payment=Loan AmountLoan Term=100,000,00036≈2,777,778 USD per month\text{Principal Payment} = \frac{\text{Loan Amount}}{\text{Loan Term}} = \frac{100,000,000}{36} \approx 2,777,778 \text{ USD per month}Principal Payment=Loan TermLoan Amount​=36100,000,000​≈2,777,778 USD per month

Step 2: Calculate Monthly Interest Payment​

For the first month, the interest payment can be calculated using the initial full loan amount:

Interest Payment=(Loan Amount×Annual Interest Rate12)=(100,000×0.0812)≈666,667 USD for the first month\text{Interest Payment} = \left(\frac{\text{Loan Amount} \times \text{Annual Interest Rate}}{12}\right) = \left(\frac{100,000 \times 0.08}{12}\right) \approx 666,667 \text{ USD for the first month}Interest Payment=(12Loan Amount×Annual Interest Rate​)=(12100,000×0.08​)≈666,667 USD for the first month

Step 3: Calculate Total Monthly Payment​

Now, add the monthly principal and the interest payments for the total monthly payment for the first month:

Total Monthly Payment=Principal Payment+Interest Payment=2,777,778+666,667=3,444,444 USD for the first month\text{Total Monthly Payment} = \text{Principal Payment} + \text{Interest Payment} = 2,777,778 + 666,667 = 3,444,444 \text{ USD for the first month}Total Monthly Payment=Principal Payment+Interest Payment=2,777,778+666,667=3,444,444 USD for the first month
